🧠 But What is a Neural Network?

A Visual Introduction to Deep Learning

by 3Blue1Brown (Grant Sanderson)

Why Watch This Video?

This is widely considered the best introduction to neural networks available online. Grant Sanderson (3Blue1Brown) has a unique gift for making complex mathematical concepts visually intuitive and accessible to everyone—from high school students to professional researchers.

Rather than diving into intimidating equations, this video builds understanding from the ground up using beautiful animations and clear analogies. You'll see exactly how neural networks learn to recognize patterns, understand why they're structured the way they are, and gain genuine insight into what "deep learning" actually means.

No advanced mathematics required. If you can follow basic arithmetic and are willing to think visually, you'll understand neural networks by the end of this 19-minute video.

📚 After Watching

After watching this video, you'll have the conceptual foundation to understand how modern AI systems work. The transformers, diffusion models, and GANs we discussed earlier all build on these same basic principles—they're just more sophisticated arrangements of the same fundamental building blocks.

Optional: If you found this video helpful and want to go deeper, the remaining videos in the series (gradient descent, backpropagation, and what neural networks actually learn) provide even richer understanding. But this first video alone gives you 80% of what you need to be an informed AI user in research.
